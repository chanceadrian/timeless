What is Design?

Essential ideas and approaches to design, from Braun to Apple.

Chance Castaneda

PCS 52 E, Braun

Design for me is about producing orientation. It’s about giving intent to our environment and executing the best of our ideas to make us more than we are, to do bigger things, and achieve our aims.

Design is an art and a science. It’s an art because it requires us not only to see what is missing, but to know what could or should be there. This is the art component, the beauty, and emotion of design.

But, knowing what is already there, such as the way we perceive graphics, form mental models, or provide honest feedback is what makes it a science. And it is only through this interdisciplinary approach, one with room for beauty and methodology, that we may nurture, apply, and execute our ideas.

At a fundamental level, I believe design negotiates between our ideal and present environment. It’s hard to say why or how we’re capable of imagining better alternatives to what is in front of us, but we almost universally do.

To ensure our designs cause no harm in this process, we’ve got to prioritize honest deliberation at every level of resolution. For me, it’s the only way to make sure we’ve not only got it right, but that we’ve got it right for the most people possible.

We design in hopes of creating, or more likely discovering, something valuable—a technology or idea that moves us forward. And as we grow our capacity for making better things, so does our lists of principles to help get us there.

The following sections showcases some of my favorites.

Braun Design 1972

Innovative

Act as if there is always a possibility of better.

Useful

Emphasize usefulness, and satisfy functional and aesthetic needs.

Aesthetic

Execute well to be beautiful; our well being depends on it.

Understandable

Clarify structure, and be easy to explain. Be obvious.

Unobstrusive

Stay neutral and restrained, and leave room for self-expression.

Honest

Do not manipulate with promises that cannot be kept.

Long-lasting

Prepare to live many years, so avoid being fashionable.

Detailed

Leave nothing to chance. Be accurate, never arbitrary.

Sustainable

Conserve resources, and reduce visual and physical pollution.

Less, but better

Concentrate on the essential; stay pure, stay simple.

Apple Design 2017

Wayfinding

Be easy to navigate, especially in a hurry.

Feedback

Tell us when our actions had consequences.

Visibility

Make essential functions impossible to miss.

Consistency

Set expectations and stick to them everywhere.

Mental Model

Meet expectations or make new systems simple.

Proximity

Make controls next to what they change.

Grouping

Categorize by function or what makes sense.

Mapping

A slider should go up to make something raise.

Affordance

Indicate how something should be used.

Progressive Disclosure

Start simple; then iteratively go complex.

80/20 Rule

Reduce clutter. 80% benifits from 20% of actions.

Symmetry

Optimize for visual harmony.
